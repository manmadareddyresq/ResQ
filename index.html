Problem
The Emergency Gap in Indian Cities
India has ambulances, hospitals and emergency numbers ‚Äî but no unified, intelligent rail that coordinates them in real time. The result: unpredictable response and invisible failures.
Fragmented dispatch
108 / 102, hospital-owned ambulances, and private operators all run their own routing. No single ‚Äúsource of truth‚Äù for who is closest, who is free, and where traffic is blocked.
No visibility for families
Once a call is placed, most families are blind. No live map, no verified ETA, and no way to know if a better ambulance was available.
Trust & pricing issues
Private ambulances are often negotiated on the road, with unverified operators and inconsistent pricing ‚Äî especially during high-stress cases.
Policy‚Äìtechnology gap
Governments want data to improve response, but modern, neutral, tech-first rails are missing. Existing pilots are often siloed, poorly designed, or non-scalable.
Solution
ResQ: A Neutral, AI-First Emergency Rail
ResQ is a neutral coordination layer that routes government and private ambulances like packets on a network: fastest, safest, verifiable.
Layer 1
Citizen & family app
SOS, hospital selection guidance, real-time tracking, live ETA, emergency contact notifications and digital receipts after the trip.
Layer 2
Driver / operator app
Optimised pick-up and drop routes, single-tap status updates, integrated navigation and trip history with SLA metrics.
Layer 3
Control & analytics dashboard
City heatmaps, bottleneck detection, fleet utilisation analytics and policy-friendly data exports for health departments.
Layer 4
AI decision engine
Predictive routing, ETA correction, demand forecasting and triage-based prioritisation to keep the platform learning from every trip.

Risk & Learnings
Why Others Struggled ‚Äî and What We Learn
ResQ is not positioned as ‚Äúone more ambulance aggregator‚Äù. We treat failed and partial attempts in this space as lessons, not direct competition.
Lesson 1: Pure B2C without system integration struggles
Many apps tried to be ‚ÄúUber for ambulances‚Äù without integrating with hospitals, 108/102 or local health authorities. ResQ is being designed as a system rail, not just an app.
Lesson 2: Tech without operations fails
Some players launched good apps but weak field operations and onboarding, leading to poor supply. Our roadmap explicitly includes operations, SLAs and KYC for every partner.
Lesson 3: Over-optimistic national launch
Rapid multi-city expansion without depth has hurt previous attempts. ResQ‚Äôs plan is to go deep in Hyderabad first and prove operational excellence before expansion.
Strategic position: Unique, but not naive
We acknowledge that emergency rails are hard. Our advantage is starting focused, AI-first, and co-designing with operators instead of treating them as an afterthought.
Risk Aware ‚Ä¢ Learning Driven
ResQ‚Äôs design, from first prototype, is built around these learnings ‚Äî with clear checkpoints: pilots, operator NPS, SLA adherence, and collaboration readiness with state health bodies.
