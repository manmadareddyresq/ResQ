Founder Story

Manmada Reddy — Former B.Tech CSE (AI/ML) student at LPU. Dropped out in 2nd year, restarted studies with BBA at UCMH, JNTUH. Growing up in Hyderabad, I repeatedly saw ambulances stuck in traffic while patients deteriorated. That injustice sparked ResQ.

Why me? Technical background in AI/ML, operational focus, and lived exposure to the emergency gaps in Indian cities. I built this project with a simple aim: reduce preventable deaths by getting help to people faster.

Vision & Mission

Vision:
  • India's first unified, AI-driven emergency response ecosystem.
  • Instant requests and reliable tracking across government & private fleets.
Mission:
  • Reduce emergency response time using AI for routing, demand forecasting and automated triage.
  • Bring transparency, verified partners, and measurable outcomes to emergency response.

Problem Statement

  • No real-time ambulance/fire tracking in most Indian cities.
  • Long delays in manual dispatching (108/102).
  • Unclear choices and unregulated private pricing.
  • Poor visibility for families — causing panic and preventable deaths.

Our Solution

  • One app: Ambulance, Fire & Rescue — live GPS, ETA, verified providers.
  • AI-first features: predictive routing, ETA optimization, demand forecasting, automated triage, and fraud detection.
  • Hybrid model: Govt (free) + Private (paid) with transparent pricing and invoicing.

AI Features (Differentiator)

  • Predictive Routing: AI finds fastest path considering live traffic, roadblocks, and historical delay patterns.
  • ETA Optimization: ML models continuously improve ETAs using live telemetry from vehicles.
  • Automated Triage: NLP-based short triage to prioritise critical cases and trigger correct vehicle type.
  • Demand Forecasting: Anticipate hotspots & preposition vehicles during events or peak hours.
  • Fraud & Quality Detection: Automatically flag suspicious pricing or unverified operators.

12‑Month Roadmap

  1. Months 0–3: Build Android MVP, onboarding 30–50 ambulances in pilot city, basic AI routing prototype.
  2. Months 3–6: Launch pilot in Hyderabad, gather trip data, refine ETA model, sign 1–2 hospital partners.
  3. Months 6–9: Add payments, multilingual support, improve AI models, start govt liaison for 108/102 data sharing.
  4. Months 9–12: Scale to 2–3 additional cities, introduce hospital dashboards & subscription pilots.
