Deep dive ¬∑ Problem framing

The Emergency Reality Why ResQ needs to exist

In a medical emergency in Hyderabad today, a family typically: dials 108 or a hospital, tries private contacts, waits without visibility, and spends the most stressful minutes of their life guessing.

1. No live visibility

Families don't know if an ambulance has actually started, from where, or what the ETA is. Many end up standing on the road refreshing other map apps without seeing the actual vehicle.

2. Fragmented ecosystem

‚Ä¢ Government 108/102
‚Ä¢ Hospital-owned ambulances
‚Ä¢ Independent operators

All run separately, with different phone numbers, priorities and minimal data sharing.

3. Manual dispatch = slow

Dispatch centers still rely on voice, pen and paper. One congested phone line can delay multiple assignments. No automatic load balancing or ‚Äúnearest optimal‚Äù calculation.

4. Trust gap in private options

Pricing is often opaque, with fear of overcharging. Families hesitate to call private ambulances unless they have a trusted reference ‚Äî wasting precious minutes.

Time is the real currency in emergencies

‚Ä¢ Beyond ~8‚Äì10 minutes in certain emergencies (cardiac arrest, trauma), survival probabilities drop dramatically.
‚Ä¢ Yet typical urban response in unorganised systems easily crosses 15‚Äì20 minutes, especially during traffic peaks.

Deep dive ¬∑ Product & user journey

ResQ Product Journey From SOS to arrival

ResQ is designed as a clean, minimal interface on top of a complex routing brain. For the user, it feels as simple as: Tap ‚Üí Select ‚Üí Track.

1. App entry & roles

‚Ä¢ Register, Login, or Continue as Guest.
‚Ä¢ Guest users can transparently view hospital options and pricing, but cannot book ‚Äî they see value without risk of spam.
‚Ä¢ Registered users can save emergency contacts, medical notes and preferences.

2. Request creation

‚Ä¢ Location is auto-detected and not manually editable to avoid misuse.
‚Ä¢ Destination choice is limited to verified hospitals, with ratings and basic comparison.
‚Ä¢ A single, clear question appears: ‚ÄúIs the patient conscious?‚Äù
This feeds into triage priority and dispatch logic.

3. Intelligent dispatch

‚Ä¢ ResQ finds the best available option across:
  ‚Äì Government 108 (non-commercial, subject to collaboration)
  ‚Äì Private basic ambulances
  ‚Äì ICU / advanced ambulances

Government options are clearly marked as ‚Äúsubject to live integration‚Äù, while private fleets show per-km pricing and ETA.

4. Live tracking & communication

‚Ä¢ Family sees vehicle on map with ETA and driver name + rating.
‚Ä¢ One-tap buttons: Call driver, Share live link, Notify emergency contact.
‚Ä¢ Emergency contacts receive an auto-generated SMS / WhatsApp style text with location and ETA.

5. Completion & summary

‚Ä¢ Once marked ‚ÄúCompleted‚Äù, ResQ shows a clear trip summary:
 ‚Äì Time of request, time of assignment, arrival time
 ‚Äì Approx distance and calculated fare (for private)
 ‚Äì Option to email PDF invoice to the user.

Over time, this becomes a health mobility logbook for the family.

6. Learning loop

‚Ä¢ Every trip feeds into AI routing models ‚Äî what worked, what delayed.
‚Ä¢ The system gets smarter for specific localities, signals, and peak hours.
‚Ä¢ For partners, ResQ can share anonymised insights: ‚Äúthis junction adds +3.5 minutes on average at 8‚Äì10 pm‚Äù.

Deep dive ¬∑ Risk & learning

Risk & Learnings from Past Attempts Not ‚Äúcompetitors‚Äù ‚Äî references

ResQ is not positioned as ‚Äúanother ambulance app‚Äù. Earlier players in adjacent areas struggled for different reasons: business model, adoption friction, or tech reliability. We see them as lessons, not current competitors.

What existed What went wrong What ResQ learns
City-based ambulance call centres & basic apps Trust issues, app crashes, inconsistent response. Focus on reliability first; keep flow extremely simple and under-promise, over-deliver on ETA.
Premium B2B ambulance services Focused mainly on corporate / hospital contracts, not the average citizen in need. ResQ starts with the citizen experience and then plugs into hospitals and government as a neutral layer.
Govt-only, call-based 108/102 setups Powerful, but limited visibility, no public tracking, and sometimes overload during peaks. ResQ is designed to augment this ‚Äî not replace ‚Äî by offering UI, analytics and optional integration points.

Instead of labelling others as ‚Äúcompetition‚Äù, ResQ is built with a risk-aware mindset: understand where attempts failed, and build guardrails into product, operations and communication.
